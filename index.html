Buyer’s Guide

Meal Variety


When selecting a meal kit service, variety is the spice of life. Look for services that offer a rotating menu every week so you don't get bored of eating the same stir-fry or salmon for months. A good service will offer diverse cuisines, including Italian, Mexican, and Asian-inspired dishes. For those following the best gluten-free meal kits under $200 per month route, ensure the service provides enough different protein options (chicken, beef, fish, tofu) to keep your diet balanced.

Dietary Restrictions & Safety


This is the most critical factor. If you have celiac disease, cross-contamination is a serious risk. Avoid services like Freshly, Dinnerly, or Hungryroot that do not have dedicated gluten-free labeling or kitchens. Services like Sun Basket and Home Chef have better protocols for labeling wheat-free meals, though cross-contamination can still occur during shipping. Always verify if the meal plan is "Certified Gluten-Free" if that is your medical requirement.

Shipping Options


Shipping costs can significantly impact your total monthly bill. Some services offer free shipping on subscriptions over a certain dollar amount, while others charge a flat fee or have location-based restrictions. Factor and Sun Basket ship primarily to the contiguous U.S. Before subscribing, check if they deliver to your specific zip code and what the shipping policy entails. Weekend delivery can be an issue for some services, so ensure your delivery day aligns with your schedule.

Price Per Meal


Calculating the price per meal helps you determine if a service fits your under $200 per month budget. Most services cost between $5 and $15 per serving. A standard week usually consists of four to five meals. Therefore, a service at $10 per meal might cost you around $200 for a week of meals. However, remember to factor in shipping fees and potential taxes. Factor Meal Delivery is consistently on the higher end ($15/meal), while Dinnerly is on the lower end ($4.99-$11), allowing for more frequent meals within a tighter budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are gluten-free meal kits?
Gluten-free meal kits are subscription services that deliver pre-portioned ingredients and recipes specifically designed to exclude wheat, barley, and rye. These services take the stress out of grocery shopping and recipe creation by providing everything you need to cook a safe, delicious meal without gluten.

How do I choose the best gluten-free meal kit?
The best kit depends on your budget, lifestyle, and dietary severity. If you need strict safety, look for services with dedicated gluten-free menus (like Sun Basket). If you have a lower budget, Home Chef or Dinnerly might be better, but you must verify ingredients yourself. Consider whether you want to cook (kits) or just heat up (pre-prepared) meals.

Are these meal kits suitable for celiac disease?
Not all services are suitable for celiac disease. Services that are specifically labeled "gluten-free" or offer a dedicated gluten-free meal plan are safer options. However, even then, it is best practice to contact the company to ask about their cross-contamination protocols in their kitchens and packaging.

What is the average cost of gluten-free meal kits?
The average cost typically ranges from $9 to $15 per serving. With a standard meal plan of four to five meals per week, this can add up to $160 to $300 per month. To find the best gluten-free meal kits under $200 per month, you will likely need to stick to the lower end of the price range, such as Home Chef or Dinnerly, provided you perform your own due diligence on ingredients.

How often can I receive my meal kits?
Most meal kit services operate on a weekly delivery cycle. You can typically choose your delivery day (e.g., Sunday or Monday) and the frequency (once a week, once every two weeks). If you have a subscription, you can pause or cancel the service at any time, depending on the company's specific policy.
